Genetic Counseling Exercise A : Pedigree construction and probability calculation

Miles and Julia have been married for 3 years and are thinking of starting a family. But they want to talk to a genetic counselor to help them determine their risks of having a child with a serious illness. Miles and Julia met because of a genetic illness – hemophilia A. Hemophilia A is a serious illness in which the blood of an affected person will not clot correctly due to a lack of functional clotting factor VIII. Even minor cuts and injuries can be serious because these people bleed very heavily. In the past, hemophilia was a kind of death sentence because no treatments existed but now, patients with hemophilia A can be treated by regular injection with clotting factor. Neither Miles nor Julia has hemophilia A, but each has a brother with it. Now that they are considering having children, they are understandably concerned about the chances that one of their children might be affected with this serious disease.

A genetic counselor will first take a family history – find out as much as possible about the family members in order to estimate as accurately as possible the odds of having a child affected by these disorders. In this exercise you will act as the genetic counselor, looking at the family information, making a pedigree for Miles and Julia and finally making some probability calculations to help them understand their risks of having a child affected with one or both of the disorders they are worried about.
